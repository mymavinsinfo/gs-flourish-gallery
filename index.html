Commuting around Australia

Let's take a look at commutes around the country.

Hobart is the most expensive capital city for commuting at $22.38.

Sydney has the longest average commute time at 62 minutes.

While Brisbane has the longest commute distance of any capital city at 32km.

We're now back in Hobart for the shortest distance commute in a captial city at 26km.

While Darwin has both the cheapest average commute at $17 and the shortest commute time at 36 minutes out of all the capital cities.

Australians are spending a lot of time commuting, on average 54 minutes per day!

That adds up to roughly 169 hours per person every year!

The average daily cost of commuting per person ranges from $9 to $27.

Across the nation, this averages to about $20 daily.

Or $104 weekly...

...which totals a whopping $4,003 per year!

That’s a lot of money, particuarly with rising financial concerns and a potentially looming recession.

It’s not surprising that most commuters surveyed (85%) agree the government could do more to ease the costs.

The research shows that almost 7 in 10 (68%) agree that travel time and costs strongly impact their choice of job and work location.

Nearly 3 in 4 (73%) agree that they would change jobs if there were work oppourtinities close to home.

More than 8 in 10 (83%) of commuters agree that commuting to work has never been as expensive as it is now!
